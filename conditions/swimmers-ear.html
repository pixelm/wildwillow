Swimmer's ear

also called otitis externa

An itchy, sometimes painful ear after swimming or bathing. In Ayurveda this maps to karna kandu — vitiated kapha trapped in the canal. The aim is to dry the moisture, calm the inflammation, and restore the ear's natural oil barrier.

Kapha Mild Kids 5+ & adults

Right now

  • Stop swimming until 2 days after pain ends
  • Keep the ear dry — no soap or water in the canal
  • Sleep with the affected ear up

Recovery

  1. Day 1–2Begin oil drops
  2. Day 3–4Pain easing
  3. Day 5–7Resolution + 2 dry days

How the four modalities approach this

Ayurveda

Trapped kapha — moisture in the canal that needs to be dried and warmed.

Western herbalism

The boundary between minor inflammation and bacterial colonization — soothe and protect.

Yoga

The ear-sinus passages share one drainage system — gentle inversions and openers move what's stuck.

Breathwork

Skull resonance through humming reaches deeper than oils — vibration shifts trapped moisture.

Where they meet and diverge. All four agree that drying and protecting the canal matters more than killing pathogens — which is unusual; conventional medicine reaches for antibiotic drops first. Where they diverge: Ayurveda emphasizes the sesame oil barrier, Western herbalism the antimicrobial garlic. Yoga and breathwork see the condition as connected to broader ENT health; the substance modalities treat it locally.

When to choose what

  • First time this season, mild itch, no pain

    Start with the Ayurveda or herbalism top pick plus the "Right now" actions.

  • Recurring after every swim session this summer

    Add daily Bhramari as prevention; consider nasya with Anu Taila as a longer protocol.

  • Pain that's getting worse, or third recurrence this summer

    This is past home-remedy territory — book an integrative practitioner.

  • Already tried oils for 3 days with no shift

    Time to escalate, not double down — see your pediatrician.

What we don't recommend

  • Hydrogen peroxide drops

    Dry out the canal lining and can worsen the underlying issue.

  • Cotton swabs

    Push debris and wax deeper, raising infection risk.

  • Antibiotic drops for first-time cases

    Overtreatment for a usually self-limiting condition. Reserve for recurrence or worsening.

  • Homeopathic ear products

    No consistent evidence base for this condition; we'd rather not recommend than recommend without basis.

See a doctor if

Fever, discharge, hearing loss, severe pain, or no improvement in 3 days. Don't apply oil if you suspect a ruptured eardrum.
